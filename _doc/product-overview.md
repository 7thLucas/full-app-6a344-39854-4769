# Product Overview: FlowerOps

## What It Is
A web app for florist shop owners to manage the complete operational cycle of their shop — tracking perishable flower inventory, fulfilling bouquet orders, and scheduling deliveries — with every feature oriented toward minimizing spoilage and delivering fresh arrangements on time.

## Users & Personas

**Primary — Shop Owner**
Independent florist running a small-to-medium shop (1–10 staff). Wears every hat: sourcing, arranging, customer service, admin. Needs fast, reliable visibility into what's in stock, what orders need filling, and what goes out today. Time is the scarcest resource.

**Secondary — Shop Manager / Senior Florist**
Delegated operator. Manages day-to-day fulfillment when the owner isn't on the floor. Needs the same operational clarity without the broader business context.

## Core Problem
Floristry runs on perishable, time-sensitive stock. Without a dedicated system:
- Inventory is tracked by memory or whiteboard — spoilage goes unnoticed until stems are already unusable
- Every wilted stem is unrecoverable margin loss (typical spoilage rates in small florist shops run 10–20% of purchased inventory)
- Orders managed in notebooks and group chats lead to fulfillment errors and missed specs
- Delivery scheduling is ad hoc — drivers get conflicting instructions, customers wait, and trust erodes
- Missing a delivery window for a wedding or funeral is not just a refund — it is a permanently lost relationship

## Solution
A single, focused operations dashboard that:
1. Tracks flower inventory with freshness windows and smart reorder alerts
2. Manages bouquet orders from intake to fulfillment across their full lifecycle
3. Schedules and monitors deliveries with driver assignment and real-time status
4. Surfaces spoilage risk proactively — matching at-risk stems to pending orders before they turn

## Core Features

### 1. Flower Inventory Manager
Track species, quantities, supplier, purchase date, freshness expiry, and minimum stock thresholds. Alerts fire when stock approaches the spoilage window (1–3 days) or falls below the reorder point. Every stem is accounted for from arrival to use.

### 2. Bouquet Order Management
Create and track orders with customer details, arrangement specifications, required flower varieties, and status progression: Pending → In Progress → Ready → Delivered. Due dates are visible across all orders to prevent scheduling conflicts.

### 3. Delivery Schedule
Calendar-based delivery scheduling with assigned driver/courier, delivery address, customer contact, time window, and real-time status tracking: Scheduled → In Transit → Delivered. Eliminates verbal handoffs and conflicting instructions.

### 4. Spoilage Prevention Dashboard
The shop's financial safety net: an at-a-glance view of inventory expiring within 1–3 days, with smart suggestions to match those stems to open orders. Converts what was silent loss into recoverable revenue.

## Target Outcomes (Stated Goals)
- Reduce weekly spoilage loss by up to 30% through proactive freshness alerts and order matching
- Achieve 95%+ on-time delivery rate through structured scheduling and status visibility
- Save shop owners and staff approximately 8 hours per week of manual tracking, order chasing, and delivery coordination
- Reach near-zero order errors by centralizing all order details and specs in one place

## Strategic Principles
- **Freshness-first**: Every feature decision should help flowers reach customers sooner, not just be recorded.
- **Minimal friction**: Shop owners are on their feet, not at a desk. Interactions must be fast, touch-friendly, and task-complete in seconds.
- **Profit protection**: Spoilage reduction is the #1 ROI driver; surface it on the dashboard's primary view.

## Brand & Tone
- Warm, calm, professional — like a well-run shop, not a startup dashboard
- Uses florist language throughout: stems, arrangements, bouquets, deliveries — not "items", "tickets", "shipments"
- Visual palette: fresh botanical greens, blush rose, clean white — nature-grounded, not sterile
- Not tech-forward; operationally focused for people who work with their hands

## Scope (MVP)
Web app. Four core modules: Inventory Manager, Order Management, Delivery Schedule, Spoilage Prevention Dashboard. Out of scope for V1: supplier ordering, customer-facing portal, e-commerce integration, POS integration.

## North Star Assumptions (for projections)
- Typical small florist shop: 25–40 bouquet orders per week
- Average flower COGS per order: ~$30
- Typical pre-app spoilage rate: 15% of purchased inventory value
- Achievable spoilage reduction with the app: ~50% of current rate
- Admin time currently spent on manual tracking, order coordination, delivery scheduling: ~8 hrs/week
- Staff hourly cost for admin work: ~$18/hr
